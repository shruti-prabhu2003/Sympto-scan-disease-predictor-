Breast Cancer

Breast cancer originates in the cells of the breast tissue. It occurs when the cells in the breast grow uncontrollably and form a tumor. Breast cancer can affect both men and women, but it is much more common in women.

Diabetes

Diabetes mellitus, commonly referred to as diabetes, is a chronic metabolic disorder that affects how your body uses glucose (sugar), the primary source of energy for cells. Diabetes can be type-1 (genetic) or type-2 (lifestyle factors).

Chronic-Kidney Disease

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is a long-term, progressive condition in which the kidneys gradually lose their ability to function properly. This deterioration can eventually lead to kidney failure if left untreated or poorly managed.
